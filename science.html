UCLCHEM


Most of my work centres around chemical modelling and use of my chemical code UCLCHEM. This is a code written in Modern Fortran based on the original code by Serena Viti with contributions from many authors.


UCLCHEM is very much an all purpose chemical solver and was developed to model clouds, shocks and hot cores; it is easily extendible to other applications. I'm happy to work with anyone who wants to use the code otherwise it is freely available on Github.


Statistical Work


In my current PDRA position at Leiden Observatory, I work on the application of "big data" techniques to extra-galactic chemistry. For example, my current work includes the creation of emulators to bypass the complexity of my chemical model for parameter inference or for use as a subgrid model.


I also make use of Bayesian inference and MCMC techniques try to infer real physical and chemical parameters from data such as the cosmic ionization rate in molecular clouds external galaxies or using measurements of ice mantle abundances in molecular clouds to constrain the rates of a highly reduced surface network.


Outflows


I have occasionally strayed into the murky world of real things that are actually happening. I've published two papers on sulfur chemistry in L1157-B1, the second of which combines my own analysis with other published work to summarize the sulfur bearing molecule content of the region.


I followed this up with a paper on methanol and acetaldehyde observations in eight outflow sources. The observations were actually taken to catch as many sulfur bearing species as possible but my ORBYTS students discovered a lot of lines of these organic molecules. We confirmed the widely assumed fact that methanol forms on ice grains and found evidence that the same is true of acetaldehyde.
