Education & Skills

I've spent an awful lot of time studying physics. I graduated from the Physics MSci course at Imperial College London in 2014 with a first and moved to University College London for my PhD. Since then, I've been working on chemistry in the places where stars form. I've spent the time developing my data analysis skills and redeveloping the Fortran code my research group uses to model chemistry. I've become comfortable in Python, C++ and Fortran as a direct result of my physics training, using these languages for many serious applications. However, student life gives plenty of opportunity to improve in other ways and I've produced working end products in a variety of languages.
